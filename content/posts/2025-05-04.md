## Model Releases

- **[Mistral-9B-Instruct-v0.4](https://huggingface.co/mistralai/Mistral-9B-Instruct-v0.4)** ⭐ — The Rebel Alliance of open-source models drops another update. At 9B parameters this is the scrappy X-wing that keeps punching above its weight class: strong instruction following, MIT licensed, runs on consumer hardware. The Empire (closed APIs) remains unimpressed, which is probably a good sign.

- **[Qwen2.5-Coder-7B](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen2.5-Coder-7B)** — Alibaba's coding model in 7B form. If Mistral is an X-wing, this is a B-wing: specialized, surprisingly capable, and slightly underrated. Apache 2.0. Strong on Python and SQL; weaker on the obscure stuff, which is true of most droids too.

- **[SmolLM2-1.7B-Instruct](https://huggingface.co/HuggingFaceTB/SmolLM2-1.7B-Instruct)** 🤖 — The R2-D2 of this digest: tiny, surprisingly useful, and occasionally incomprehensible. 1.7B parameters, runs on anything with a battery. Perfect for edge deployment or when you want a model that fits in the overhead compartment.

## Research Worth Reading

- **[LoRA-XS: Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning at 0.01% Scale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.17484)** — These are not the gradients you're looking for. LoRA-XS achieves fine-tuning with 100x fewer parameters than standard LoRA by decomposing the weight update matrices further. Results hold surprisingly well on standard benchmarks. If you're paying for compute, this paper is worth your time before your next fine-tuning run.

- **[The Phantom Benchmark: Measuring What LLMs Actually Know vs. What They've Memorized](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.11493)** — A paper that asks the uncomfortable question: how much of MMLU performance is memorization of the test set? Answer: more than comfortable. They propose a contamination-resistant evaluation protocol. Required reading before you trust any leaderboard number ever again.

## AI Dev Tools

- **[LitServe: Lightning-Fast LLM Serving](https://github.com/Lightning-AI/LitServe)** 🛠️ — Lightning AI's new inference server. Built on FastAPI, handles batching and streaming, deploys with one command. The Millennium Falcon of serving frameworks: looks rough around the edges, but it's got it where it counts. Benchmarks show it beating vLLM on small batch latency for certain workloads. Worth benchmarking against your current setup.

- **[Outlines 0.1.0](https://github.com/outlines-dev/outlines)** — Structured text generation library hits a stable release. Forces LLM outputs to conform to JSON schemas, regex patterns, or Pydantic models. This is the targeting computer you always wanted: lets the model focus on content while you control the shape. Works with llama.cpp, transformers, and the major APIs.

## MCP Servers & Integrations

- **[mcp-filesystem-extended](https://github.com/wonderwhy-er/DesktopCommanderMCP)** — MCP server that gives your coding assistant read/write access to the local filesystem with configurable sandboxing. Use The Force (responsibly): point it at a project directory and watch it actually execute your refactor instructions instead of just describing them.

## Today's Synthesis

⭐ **May the inference be with you.**

[LoRA-XS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.17484) and [SmolLM2-1.7B](https://huggingface.co/HuggingFaceTB/SmolLM2-1.7B-Instruct) point at the same thing from different angles: the real frontier right now isn't making models bigger, it's making them more deployable. LoRA-XS cuts the cost of adaptation to near-zero; SmolLM2 cuts the cost of the model itself to near-zero. Stack them together — fine-tune a 1.7B model for your domain using LoRA-XS — and you have a specialized inference pipeline that runs on a Raspberry Pi.

Meanwhile, [LitServe](https://github.com/Lightning-AI/LitServe) gives you the serving layer. The complete stack — domain-tuned tiny model + efficient serving — is now genuinely free and genuinely fast. Three months ago this required a Jedi. Today it's a weekend afternoon.

Use [Outlines](https://github.com/outlines-dev/outlines) to lock the output shape and you've got a production-grade extraction pipeline for approximately zero dollars. That's the rebellion winning.
