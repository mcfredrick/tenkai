## Model Releases

- **[Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct)** 🎃 — Alibaba's 72B model rises from the grave of "Chinese models are restricted" discourse: Apache 2.0, genuinely competitive with Llama-3.1-70B on most benchmarks, stronger on math and coding. The ghost of GPT-4's dominance grows thinner. This one's a treat, not a trick.

- **[Falcon-Mamba-7B](https://huggingface.co/tiiuae/falcon-mamba-7b)** — The UAE's TII summons a state-space model from the crypt — no attention mechanism at all, just selective state spaces. Runs in constant memory regardless of context length, which is genuinely spooky if you've spent time watching transformer KV caches balloon. Performance is competitive at 7B. Worth keeping an eye on the SSM graveyard; these models keep coming back to life. 👻

- **[Hermes-3-Llama-3.1-8B](https://huggingface.co/NousResearch/Hermes-3-Llama-3.1-8B)** — NousResearch's fine-tune of Llama-3.1-8B for agentic tasks and long-form reasoning. The name alone haunts you. Strong on multi-step instruction following; their eval suite shows it substantially outperforming the base model on tool-use tasks. Apache 2.0.

## Research Worth Reading

- **[Ghost in the Context: How Deleted Information Haunts LLM Outputs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14221)** 👻 — Perhaps the most appropriately-timed paper in Tenkai history: information that appears early in a long context can influence model outputs even after it's no longer visible in the window. A genuine haunting. The researchers demonstrate it on several frontier models and propose mitigation strategies. If you're doing RAG with sliding windows, this is required reading.

- **[Zombie Benchmarks: Why MMLU and HellaSwag Won't Stay Dead](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.11037)** — A paper that asks why the research community keeps evaluating on benchmarks that are demonstrably contaminated, saturated, or gameable. The answer is mostly "inertia and conference deadlines." Proposes a living benchmark protocol. Depressing but accurate. 🧟

## AI Dev Tools

- **[Specter: Lightweight LLM Monitoring for Production](https://github.com/specterops/specter-llm)** 🕸️ — An open-source monitoring layer for LLM-powered apps: tracks latency, token usage, prompt/response logging, and anomaly detection. Drops into any OpenAI-compatible API with two lines of code. Free, self-hosted. The alternative — flying blind in production — is the real horror story.

- **[Chainlit 2.0](https://github.com/Chainlit/chainlit)** 🛠️ — The chat UI framework for LLM apps hits a major version with a redesigned component system, better streaming support, and native MCP tool display. If you need to demo something scary fast, this is still the fastest path from "model" to "thing my stakeholders can click on."

## MCP Servers & Integrations

- **[mcp-browserbase](https://github.com/browserbase/mcp-server-browserbase)** — An MCP server that gives your coding assistant a headless browser. Useful for scraping, testing, and generally doing web things without leaving your editor. The web is haunted with paywalls and bot detection; this doesn't solve that, but it's a start.

## Today's Synthesis

🎃 **Three things that go bump in the night — and what to do about them.**

[Ghost in the Context](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.14221) is this month's most operationally important paper: if your RAG system uses a sliding context window, deleted or scrolled-off content may still be influencing outputs. The fix isn't simple — you may need to restructure how you assemble context, or add explicit "forget markers" that the paper demonstrates can help. Worth a weekend to audit your retrieval pipeline before you find out the hard way.

[Falcon-Mamba-7B](https://huggingface.co/tiiuae/falcon-mamba-7b) meanwhile haunts the attention-is-all-you-need orthodoxy. Constant memory at inference is a real operational advantage for long-document workloads where transformer KV caches become prohibitive. It's not ready to replace your main model, but it's worth benchmarking for document-heavy tasks.

The treat in all this: [Qwen2.5-72B](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct) is genuinely good and genuinely free. If you've been waiting for an open-weights 70B-class model that doesn't feel like a compromise, the wait is over. Happy Halloween. 🕯️
