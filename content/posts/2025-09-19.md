## Model Releases

- **[Llama-3.2-11B-Vision-Instruct](https://huggingface.co/meta-llama/Llama-3.2-11B-Vision-Instruct)** 🏴‍☠️ — Arrr, Meta has dropped a vision model off the plank and into the open sea, matey! This 11B vessel can parse images as well as text, and she sails under the Llama 3.2 Community License, which be free enough for most buccaneers. Benchmark scores would shiver yer timbers compared to what the closed-source navy was chargin' for a year ago.

- **[Phi-3.5-MoE-Instruct](https://huggingface.co/microsoft/Phi-3.5-MoE-instruct)** — Microsoft launches a Mixture-of-Experts galleon with 16 experts and 6.6B active parameters per forward pass. She be fast — only activates a portion of her crew for each token, like a ship that only rouses the sailors it needs for each maneuver. MIT licensed. Handles long context better than most ships her size. ⚓

- **[Mistral-Nemo-Instruct-2409](https://huggingface.co/mistralai/Mistral-Nemo-Instruct-2409)** — A freshly careened hull on the Nemo, with improved instruction following and reduced hallucination rates. Apache 2.0, 12B parameters. In pirate terms: fewer treasure maps to nowhere.

## Research Worth Reading

- **[Sparse Attention for Long-Context Reasoning: Navigating the Seven Seas of Tokens](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.08821)** 📄 — A paper that tackles the kraken of long-context inference: attention that scales quadratically be dragging every ship to the bottom. These researchers plot a course through sparse attention patterns that maintain quality on needle-in-haystack tasks while cutting compute by 60%. Ye won't want to miss this one if yer models handle long documents.

- **[Why Do LLMs Lie? A Taxonomy of Hallucination Types](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.09144)** — ARRR, why do the blasted models make things up, ye ask?! This crew has mapped out seven distinct types of hallucination — intrinsic, extrinsic, factual, temporal, and three more besides. Each type has different causes and different mitigations. Worth more than all the gold in Tortuga for anyone building production RAG systems.

## AI Dev Tools

- **[DSPy 3.0: The Optimizer's Optimizer](https://github.com/stanfordnlp/dspy)** 🛠️ — Shiver me timbers, DSPy has shipped a major release. The framework for programming (not prompting) LLMs now includes multi-stage optimization, better module composition, and MIPRO v2 as the default optimizer. If ye be hand-crafting prompts, ye be swabbing the deck with a toothbrush when DSPy is a mop.

- **[LangGraph 0.2](https://github.com/langchain-ai/langgraph)** — The stateful agent graph framework drops a significant update: better interrupt-and-resume handling, native support for human-in-the-loop workflows, and improved streaming. Good for building agents that can pause mid-plunder and wait for a human to approve the next move. ⚓

- **[ChromaDB 0.5](https://github.com/chroma-core/chroma)** — The vector database releases a 2x speedup on ANN queries and adds filtering on nested metadata. Arrr, yer retrieval pipeline just got a new wind in its sails.

## MCP Servers & Integrations

- **[mcp-perplexity](https://github.com/wysh3/perplexity-mcp)** 🦜 — An MCP server that routes search queries through Perplexity's API from yer coding assistant. Give yer parrot access to the whole internet and it'll stop making things up. Mostly.

## Community Finds
